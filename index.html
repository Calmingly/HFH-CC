Order MyChart Flowsheets for Patient-Entered Results

Order a MyChart Flowsheet during an office visit so patients can enter their own testing results from MyChart.

Providers / RN / MA Version 2.0 Last Updated June 2022

Order the Flowsheet in Epic

  1. In the Orders Preference section at the bottom of the visit, type MyChart in the Search for New Order field and click Search. Orders Preference section with MyChart search
  2. Select the flowsheet option(s) you want the patient to enter data for (e.g., "MyChart Glucose Flowsheet") and click Accept. MyChart flowsheet options available for selection
  3. Click the blue Summary Sentence to edit the order details:
    1. Answer notification questions — such as when you want to be notified of the patient's data entries.
    2. Enter patient instructions in the comments field. These will display to the patient in MyChart.
    3. Click Accept when complete, then Sign the order.
    Summary Sentence edit with notification and instructions fields

Patient Experience in MyChart

  1. The patient receives a new message that a flowsheet is available.
  2. The patient clicks on the active flowsheet to enter data.
  3. The patient clicks Add New Data and completes each prompted screen.
  4. The patient clicks Continue, reviews the entered data, then clicks Submit.

Review Patient Data in Epic

  1. Open In Basket and look in the Pt Flowsheet folder for patient-submitted entries.
  2. Click the message to view the patient's submitted entry. Click Pt Flowsheet to view all data entries for comparison.
  3. To view patient-entered data while in the patient's chart, open the Review Flowsheets activity.
  4. Select Print Flowsheet or Graph Region in the lower right corner to print or graph the data.
Use Case MyChart Flowsheets allow patients to self-report recurring results such as blood glucose or blood pressure directly in their MyChart account. Flowsheets are also available under the Track My Health section of the MyChart website.
Editing Patient Data Patients can edit or delete their entered data only before the data has been viewed by an Epic user.
Message the Patient From the In Basket message, select Msg to Pt to send the patient a message if needed.

Scheduling a MyChart Video Visit

Steps for scheduling a MyChart Video Visit and confirming patient eligibility requirements.

Front Desk / Scheduling Version 1.0 Updated November 2024

Background

A MyChart Video Visit is a virtual appointment with the provider where the patient connects remotely through the MyChart mobile app or MyChart website.

Hands-On Practice

  1. Go to the patient Appointment Desk and click Book It.
  2. Select New Video Visit [20423] or Return Video Visit [20424] as the visit type. These visit types trigger the decision tree.
  3. Complete the decision tree questions and select Apply or Continue. Pediatric questions may appear for patients under 18. If the patient is under 14 and does not have proxy access, the visit will be denied.
  4. If the patient does not have an active MyChart account, scheduling is denied. Follow the instructions to set up MyChart, then return to scheduling.
  5. If successful, proceed with scheduling the Video Visit and select Schedule.
  6. Review the Video Visit information with the patient and select Schedule to complete scheduling.

What Else Do You Need to Know

  • If your office uses Video Visit blocks, schedule the appointment only into a Video Visit block.
  • Use the earliest timeslot in the Video Visit block first to avoid negatively affecting provider clinic flow.
  • Ask for the best number to reach the patient on the day of the visit and enter it in Appointment Notes.
  • Share the MyChart Video Visit orientation card with the patient if your office supplies those items.

Pre-Visit Status Reference

The Pre-Visit Status [23170] column can be added to a private DAR to see if the patient is logged in, checked in, or waiting for the video visit. Status alerts can help identify when staff intervention may be needed.

🟢 Green Dot — Patient is on track to join the video visit.
✅ Green Check — Patient is connected to the visit.
🚩 Red Flag — Patient is not MyChart active, failed the hardware test, failed to log in to MyChart, or is late.
❓ Yellow Question Mark — Patient has not completed eCheck-in, has not started eCheck-in, or has not logged into MyChart recently.
Before You Begin
  • The patient has an active MyChart status.
  • The patient has a video-enabled device, such as a smartphone, tablet, or video-enabled computer.
  • The patient plans to be in Michigan or another approved state or territory during the video appointment.
  • Children under age 14 must have a proxy.
Important Tell the patient that video visits are time sensitive. If the patient cannot log in or connect on time, the provider may no longer be available. The patient should cancel the appointment if they cannot attend.
Tip Sheet

Scheduling a MyChart Video Visit

Schedule a virtual video visit appointment for a patient connecting remotely via the MyChart mobile app or website.

All Clinical Staff Version 1.0 Updated Pre-2026

About Video Visits

A Video Visit is a virtual appointment with the provider during which a patient connects remotely by video through the MyChart mobile app or the MyChart website.

Hands-On Practice

  1. Go to the patient's appointment desk and click Make Appt.
  2. Select either New Video Visit [20423] or Return Video Visit [20424].
  3. Choosing either visit type will fire a questionnaire to review with the patient. Complete the questionnaire with the patient and click Accept.
  4. Questionnaire responses will determine if the patient can be scheduled for a video visit. If appropriate, you will receive a message to review with the patient and can proceed with scheduling.
  5. Schedule the appointment ONLY into a time slot with a Video Visit block.
  6. Ask for the best number to reach the patient on the day of the visit. Enter it in Appointment Notes.
  7. Complete the scheduling and registration process. Also:
    1. Share the MyChart Video Visit orientation card with the patient.
    2. Share the late policy: "Video visits are time sensitive. If you are not able to log in or connect to your visit on time, the provider may no longer be available. Please cancel your appointment if you are not able to attend."
Note If answering "Yes" to "Is the patient under 18?", answers for the next two questions are required. If answering "No", the next two questions are not required.
Important If the patient is NOT appropriate for a video visit, you will receive an error message in red indicating the reason(s) the visit cannot be scheduled.
Reminder When scheduling video visits, use the earliest available time slot in the block first. Leaving early slots open risks releasing them to in-office visits, which negatively affects the provider's clinic flow.

Sending Direct Messages or Letters to Non-MyChart Patients

How to send a message or letter electronically via Epic to patients who are not enrolled in MyChart, using a secure link delivered by text or email.

Front Desk / Scheduling Version 1.0 Update April 2026

Background

Sending a message or letter to a patient who is not on MyChart can now be done electronically in Epic. A secure link is delivered to the patient via text or email. You can type a blank letter or use a template based on the situation.

Sending a Message or Letter

  1. Go to Appt Desk and search for your patient. Click the Communication Preference icon located next to the patient's picture on the storyboard.
  2. Verify the phone number and email are correct. Under Manage Text Message Consent, confirm the patient is Opted In for "Account, patient, and visit updates." Under Communication Preferences, confirm Letters is active for Text and/or Email.
  3. Close the panel to return to the patient's Appt Desk. On the toolbar, select Communications.
  4. In the Patient Encounter Selection box, choose to open a previous encounter or create a new encounter, then click Accept.
  5. Fill in the Type and Provider fields, then click Accept.
  6. On the far right side under the Communications section, click New Communication.
  7. Choose a letter template or create a blank letter. On the left side, click Patient. Locate the section indicating the patient is not MyChart active and expand it. Choose whether to Send Secure Link via Email and/or Text. Verify the letter content, then click Send Now.
Before You Begin
  • Patient must have a phone number and/or email address on file in Epic.
  • Patient must be opted in for text message consent (Account, patient, and visit updates).
  • For new Epic patients, complete the Demographics/Registration section first.
Note The patient will receive the message or letter via the delivery method selected (text or email) as a secure link they can open without a MyChart account.

Sending a Generic Communication Letter to a Patient

Create and send a patient letter from the Communications activity in an encounter or from the Appointment Desk.

All Staff Version 1.0 Updated Pre-2026

Background

Use the Communications activity to create and send a letter to a patient. The Communications activity can only be accessed in an encounter for clinical providers.

Hands-On Practice

  1. Clinical providers must be in a patient encounter to use the Communications activity. If an encounter is not open, open one for the patient.
  2. CSRs access Communications from the Appointment Desk screen.
  3. Type the patient name in the patient lookup box.
  4. Click New Communication.
  5. Use the quick buttons to select who should receive the letter, or search for additional contacts.
  6. Modify how recipients receive the communication, depending on how they are set up in Epic. Click the recipient name to see options. The patient may receive the letter through MyChart, Mail, or Print Locally. To fax, click the Free Text quick button.
  7. Click Other to select a letter template.
  8. Click in the search box, type Gen blank, and press Enter. Select the letter template from the list and press Accept.
  9. Address any SmartLists or wildcards (***) by pressing F2 on the keyboard.
  10. To finalize and send the letter, use an action button at the bottom right. Preview lets you view the letter. Pend saves it for later. Send Now marks it complete and sends it to the printer or through MyChart. Send at Sign Visit does the same after the encounter is closed. Cancel lets you start over.
  11. After the letter is sent, view it in Chart Review under the Letters tab.
Before You Begin
  • Clinical providers must be in a patient encounter to access the Communications activity.
  • CSRs use the Appointment Desk screen to access Communications.
